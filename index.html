Technical Documentation

This diagnostic platform simulates dynamic physical layer telecommunications over spatial geometries. It enforces hard physical thresholds for thermal noise (RF) and quantum limits (FSOC), alongside regulatory compliance boundaries.

RF Link Dynamics

The RF module determines signal viability against the background thermal noise floor ($kTB$). Link closure evaluates the Carrier-to-Noise Ratio ($C/N$) and Energy per Bit ($E_b/N_0$).

$$\frac{E_b}{N_0} = \mathit{EIRP} - \mathit{FSPL} + \frac{G}{T} - L_{atm} - k - 10\log_{10}(R_b)$$
  • Baseband Efficiency ($\eta_{bb}$): The product of the raw modulation bits-per-symbol ($m$) and the Forward Error Correction code rate ($r$). Modifying this alters the underlying Symbol Rate ($R_s$) required to meet your target data throughput.
  • Occupied Bandwidth ($B_{occ}$): Derived via the equation below, where $\alpha$ is the root-raised-cosine Roll-off Factor. $$B_{occ} = R_s \cdot (1 + \alpha)$$ If this footprint exceeds the Allocated Bandwidth limit, a Spectral Violation is flagged to ensure ITU-R compliance.
  • Carrier-to-Noise Ratio (C/N): Evaluated over the Nyquist noise bandwidth (which equates to $R_s$), ensuring the calculated analogue SNR strictly represents the signal matching the receiver's intermediate frequency filter constraints.

Optical (FSOC) Dynamics

The Free Space Optical Communications (FSOC) module abstains from thermal limits, instead evaluating link viability against discrete quantum thresholds and atmospheric phase distortions.

  • Fried Parameter ($r_0$): Quantifies the transverse coherence length of the atmosphere. Entered at a standard $500\text{ nm}$ reference, the engine dynamically scales this to your operational Wavelength ($\lambda$) via: $$r_0(\lambda) = r_{0,500} \left( \frac{\lambda}{500 \text{ nm}} \right)^{6/5}$$
  • Zernike Compensation: Simulates the active suppression of wavefront phase variance ($\sigma_\phi^2$). Uncompensated modes translate into a focal plane scatter, drastically reducing the Strehl Ratio ($S$) and inflicting severe fibre Coupling Loss. $$S \approx \exp(-\sigma_\phi^2) \quad \text{where} \quad \sigma_\phi^2 = 1.029 \left(\frac{D_{rx}}{r_0}\right)^{5/3}$$
  • Quantum Sensitivity: Sensitivity is determined by the minimum Photons/bit ($N_p$) required by the detection scheme. The engine translates this into an absolute power limit ($P_{req}$) using the continuous Data Rate ($R_b$): $$P_{req} = N_p \left(\frac{hc}{\lambda}\right) R_b$$
